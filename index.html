Overview

Redesigned the restaurant's digital ordering system, earning an 88% user satisfaction score (8.8/10 likelihood to use). Our QR code-based solution eliminates the frustration of waiting for waiters, provides complete ingredient transparency with customization options, and streamlines group payments, converting paper menu advocates into digital enthusiasts.

The Solution: Every table has a unique QR code. Customers scan it, instantly access the digital menu, browse with full ingredient information and customization options, order exactly what they want, handle payment individually (even in groups), and rate their experience, all without flagging down a waiter.

Problem & Research

The fundamental issue isn't that digital menus exist, it's that there's no widely adopted, well-designed digital alternative to paper menus despite paper menus having significant limitations. We conducted a questionnaire study with 16 participants across different age groups to understand both the problems with traditional paper menus and why existing digital solutions fail.

Core Issues Identified

Problems with Paper Menus
  • Waiting for waiter attention to order - awkward and time-consuming
  • Waiting for waiter to bring the bill - extends dining time unnecessarily
  • Incomplete ingredient information and customization options
  • Group payment chaos: waiting turns to pay by card, or one person pays and others owe money indefinitely
  • No clear visibility of what's in each dish or how to modify it
Why Current Digital Menus Fail
  • Cluttered interfaces causing decision fatigue (11/16 participants)
  • Static PDFs with no interactivity
  • Third-party apps requiring downloads
  • Accessibility barriers for elderly users
  • Still lack clear ingredient details and customization

"I hate trying to get the waiter's attention just to pay. And when we're in a group, someone always ends up paying for everyone and we all owe them money for weeks."

Critical Insight: The problem isn't technology adoptionit's that existing digital solutions don't solve the real pain points of traditional dining. Users tolerate paper menus not because they prefer them, but because current digital alternatives are worse. Our research revealed three major friction points: service dependency (waiting for waiters), information opacity (incomplete ingredient details and customization options), and payment complexity (especially in groups).

Solution: Key Features

QR Code Entry Point

Each table has a unique QR code. Customers scan it with their phone and instantly access the menu, no app download, no waiting for a waiter to take their order.

Complete Ingredient Transparency & Customization

Every dish displays full ingredient lists with clear allergen warnings and customization options, information that paper menus rarely provide comprehensively.

Individual Payment Handling

Each person at the table can pay for their own items through the app. No more waiting for turns at the card machine or tracking who owes whom money.

Persistent Accessibility Controls

Contrast, font size, and help visible from every screen without hunting through menus.

Built-in Feedback System

After payment, customers can rate their experience and specific dishes, providing valuable feedback to the restaurant without awkward face-to-face interactions.

High-Fidelity Design

The final design had a simple, easy-to-use layout inspired by the elegance of French restaurants, clean, balanced, and welcoming. It let diners see every ingredient and allergen clearly, customize their meals, pay separately without hassle, adjust accessibility settings anytime, and share feedback easily after eating.

Standout Insights

Service Independence Highly Valued

Users emphasized relief at not having to wait for waiter attention. The ability to order and pay on their own schedule was the most praised aspect, addressing a pain point users didn't realize could be solved.

Ingredient Transparency Builds Trust

Having complete ingredient information visible upfront reduced ordering anxiety, especially for users with dietary restrictions or preferences who usually have to ask multiple questions or make uncertain choices.

Individual Payments Eliminate Social Friction

The ability for each person to pay for their own order was universally praised by groups. It eliminated both the card queue problem and the "who owes whom" tracking issue.

QR Code Entry Removes Friction

The simple scan-to-start flow meant even first-time users could begin ordering within seconds. No app download requirement was crucial for adoption.

Ingredient Customization Unclear

Crossed-out ingredients confused users, they thought items were out of stock rather than removed from their order. The +/- counter for adding ingredients to cart lacked clear feedback.

Set Menu Flow Needed Guidance

After selecting a starter, users didn't know what to do next. Future iterations need contextual help elements and clearer progression indicators.

Reflection & Next Steps

This project reinforced that great UX design isn't about implementing the latest technology, it's about identifying why existing solutions fail and addressing root causes. The real problem wasn't that digital menus don't exist; it's that current implementations don't solve the actual pain points of traditional dining: waiting for service, lack of transparency, and payment complications.

The shift from "I prefer paper menus" to 8.8/10 likelihood to use validated our approach of identifying real problems first, then designing digital solutions that specifically address them. Users didn't reject digital menus, they rejected poorly designed ones that added friction instead of removing it.

Most importantly: inclusive design isn't about checking accessibility boxes, it's about recognizing that constraints inspire better solutions for everyone. Features designed for Martha (large text, high contrast) improved the experience for all users.

Looking forward, I recognize that a complete digital dining solution requires thinking beyond the customer interface. The next phase should include the restaurant management side, order processing, table management, and kitchen integration, to create a true ecosystem that benefits both diners and restaurant operations.

Future Enhancements

A complete solution requires building both sides of the platform, enhancing the customer experience and creating restaurant management tools.
